About

I study engineering at IIT Kharagpur. The discipline is electrical, but the orientation is broader — toward systems that operate at scales where individual components become statistical, and where the interesting questions are about structure, not components.

Astrophysics is where I keep returning. Not as escape from engineering, but as the place where thermodynamics, field theory, and computation converge around objects that are unforgiving about approximation. Stars do not tolerate bad models. That discipline carries over.

My current interests sit at the intersection of large-scale energy systems and the physical principles that constrain them — thermodynamic limits, entropy, dissipation at scale. The same mathematics that governs stellar interiors governs power grids. I find that correspondence useful to think about.

I write to clarify, not to publish. When an idea survives being written down, it was probably real.

On Writing
Precision is the only courtesy you can offer a complex idea.

I write to force commitment. Vague thinking survives conversation but collapses under a sentence. The goal is not elegance — it is a structure that holds under pressure.

The pieces I publish are the ones where I have failed enough times to believe the argument. Everything else stays in the notebook.
